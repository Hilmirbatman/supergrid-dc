Figure 1 · System architecture

One cryogenic spine: power, cooling and data.

Grid power enters as MV AC, runs through the superconducting cable as ±30 kV bipolar DC, and is rectified to the rack bus. The same closed-loop liquid nitrogen that keeps the cable superconducting is the cold source for the GPU racks, one cooling system instead of two. Monitoring data rides the power cable via PLC.

Figure 4 · Shared cooling loop

The cold side does double duty.

A single closed loop. Cold nitrogen leaves the liquefier, splits between the cable cryostat (a small parasitic load) and the rack heat exchangers (the main cooling job), then returns warm to be re-cooled. Because the loop is closed, no nitrogen is consumed. Open-loop delivered nitrogen as continuous rack cooling was evaluated and ruled out as far too expensive.
